# Session 2 - Masks and Markers

**Transcript Source:** `transcript_2026-07-14T13_30_07.150Z.json`

**In-World Date:** 144-1105, Thursday. The Solomani Party Congress and internal hearing are expected on Friday.

**Present:** [[0-31 Saichi Maru (Annette)]], [[Cass Pien (Kolb)]], [[Ciernan Seanislav (Peter)]], [[Dan Araujo (Josh)]], [[Emma Colbert (Laura)]], [[Judith Dumont (Carla)]], [[Varian Svazz (Allen)]], [[Victor Mendes (Steinbach)]], [[Zoy (Damon)]]

## Synopsis

The group woke in Emma's off-grid warehouse with their adrenaline fading and practical problems pressing in. [[0-31 Saichi Maru (Annette)]] needed a heavy charge within a day unless she avoided combat, the [[Identity Veils]] had only a short remaining lifespan, and the group still needed evidence, supplies, and a safer position.

After discussing whether to clear their names, work with [[Marcel Delphine]], flee to the [[Transstar Sector Exchange]], or leave Jardin entirely, the group split into three teams. One team reconnoitered the [[Hall des Futurs Choisis]], one team found a charging opportunity for Saichi, and one team bought or acquired survival supplies and secure communications.

At the Hall, the recon team saw [[Social Solidarity]] blocking [[SolSec]] access to the assassination scene. Emma recognized [[Inspector Marc Valois]], a SolSec investigator whose work involves corrupt alien biology and genetic crimes. Social Solidarity argued that SolSec could not be trusted because a SolSec operative had been implicated in Mercier's murder. Inside the Hall, [[Dr. Luc Moreau]] tried to send data through the plumbing in a self-moving [[Mobile Data Tube]]. The team intercepted the tube, disarmed its destructive charge, and recovered the data chip inside.

Saichi and Varian found a public shaming at the Grand Axis. The target was a teacher accused of telling students that everyone was equal and that humanity was not uniquely special. The crowd and police presence created enough distraction for Saichi to unplug a charging police mech and recharge herself for another three days.

The supply team went to [[Quartier-Sainte-Liberte]] for food, medical supplies, water containers, hygiene goods, batteries, and off-grid communications. Kiernan and the others acquired the basics, made military-grade modified burner comms, and secured a repair kit with limited-use bonuses for future mechanical repairs. They also met [[Lucin]], an armored data broker with a cybernetic third arm. Lucin offered a vehicle and future goodwill if they picked up a crate from Dome 2 of [[Les Jardins Fermes]] and delivered it during daylight.

After regrouping, the party chose to complete Lucin's errand while the veils still protected their identities. The pickup was straightforward: a worker in blue overalls handed over a golf-bag-sized crate at the agricultural dome. The drop-off, however, led to a secured residence marked for [[Chief Helene Marchand]], Chief of Staff of the President. A cybernetically enhanced butler opened the crate with a code and released a corgi from inside. The group left without incident, keeping the trike.

Back at the warehouse, Emma and Zoy reviewed the data from the mobile tube. It appears to be part of the information Mercier was going to reveal: roughly fifty years of genetic tests correlating unusual markers with seemingly psionic or otherwise extraordinary effects. The file is corrupted before its final conclusions. Known examples include Subject 18 repeatedly predicting a random matter generator and Subject 21 consistently identifying concealed playing cards. Laura's science review identified several marker notes: HX-14 is unequivocally alien, SC-9 is a Solomani genetic-code marker, MY22 would normally be treated as a duplicated genetic frailty, and PX-4 is non-Solomani human or otherwise non-Solomani genetic material. Ciernan's personal genetic papers do not show the identified markers.

## Clues and Revelations

- Social Solidarity is actively denying SolSec access to the assassination evidence.
- The official justification is that a SolSec operative was implicated in the killing, making SolSec untrustworthy.
- Emma suspects the conspirators may have used her SolSec status to keep SolSec out of the investigation.
- The Hall scientist's message said Mercier was killed because of data the scientist or his group gave him.
- The mobile tube is a deep-cover data courier designed to travel through piping, carry a data chip, and destroy its contents if intercepted.
- Mercier's data seems to concern long-term genetic-marker testing tied to extraordinary cognitive or psionic-like effects.
- HX-14 is alien genetic material.
- SC-9 is a Solomani genetic-code marker.
- MY22 appears as a duplicated genetic frailty.
- PX-4 is non-Solomani human or otherwise non-Solomani genetic material.
- Ciernan does not have the identified markers in his own genetic papers.
- The party now has modified burner comms that are difficult to intercept without triangulating their physical position.
- Lucin can connect the group to a Transstar broker for military-grade ammunition.
- The identity veils will likely last through only one more game unless renewed or replaced.
