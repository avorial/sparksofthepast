# Session 1 - Official Business

**Transcript Source:** `Stars g1.md`

## Synopsis

The group is summoned to the [[Hall des Futurs Choisis]] for official review and finds itself assembled in an unusually armed, suspiciously connected cluster. A broadcast announces that Minister [[Alain Mercier]] will reveal corruption in genetic records at the Party Congress. Moments later, Mercier is killed in front of them by shots that appear to implicate their own weapons.

The first response from [[Social Solidarity]] and the [[Home Guard]] becomes a firefight after [[Emma Colbert (Laura)]] receives a warning that the authorities intend to kill them. The group escapes using an intact walker/mech after [[0-31 Saichi Maru (Annette)]] kills its pilot, then is taken by a driverless grav speeder to [[Marcel Delphine]].

Marcel explains that the [[Jardin Syndicate]] wants the group alive because the official story may be used to blame both the characters and the Syndicate while burying Mercier's real findings. He provides [[Identity Veils]] and a one-use communicator, and offers unedited assassination footage in exchange for future cooperation.

## What Happened

Game 1 began with the characters arriving at the [[Hall des Futurs Choisis]] for official business: genetic review, financial review, military paperwork, administrative interviews, or other state procedures. The gathering quickly felt strange. Too many of the people waiting were armed, trained, connected, or already known to one another, suggesting the group had been assembled for a reason.

While they waited, a broadcast announced that Minister [[Alain Mercier]] intended to reveal corruption in Jardin's genetic records at the upcoming Party Congress. Moments later, Mercier rushed through the office area and was shot. The killing immediately looked wrong: several characters noticed that their own weapons had moved, fired, heated up, or otherwise been made to look involved. [[Ciernan Seanislav (Peter)]] found a missing shotgun round, and the party realized they were being framed for the assassination.

[[Elena Verona]] and Home Guard forces moved in to control the scene. The group initially tried to understand or comply, but [[Emma Colbert (Laura)]] received a warning through her attached computer that the authorities intended to kill them. The situation turned into a firefight. The party fought through the Home Guard response, recovered useful equipment and Mercier's locked devices, and escaped the building when [[0-31 Saichi Maru (Annette)]] killed the pilot of a walker/mech without destroying the machine.

The group used the walker to descend the outside of the building and was then collected by a driverless grav speeder. The speeder carried them through hidden service and waste-disposal routes beneath the arcology to [[Marcel Delphine]], an uplifted dolphin tied to the [[Jardin Syndicate]]. Marcel claimed the Syndicate did not kill Mercier and that Mercier's discovery pointed to corruption "from above," not simply to criminal genetic-record manipulation.

By the end of the game, the official story had already turned against the party. News broadcasts blamed genetic outcasts, used false footage of [[Zoy (Damon)]], and positioned the characters as terrorists. Marcel gave the group [[Identity Veils]] and a one-use communicator, while the party regrouped at Emma's warehouse with Mercier's locked pad, new enemies, and several possible paths: clear their names, bargain with Marcel, seek refuge at the [[Transstar Sector Exchange]], or find a way off Jardin.
