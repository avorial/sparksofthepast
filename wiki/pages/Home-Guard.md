# Home Guard

*Jardin's internal defense reserve*

## Overview

The Home Guard is a Jardin security force referenced as the expected defender against dissidents, rebel outbreaks, and revolutionaries. Veterans and retired military personnel may carry weapons with military IDs partly so they can support the Home Guard during unrest.

Home Guard personnel responded to the [[Mercier Assassination]] scene under the broader [[Social Solidarity]] containment effort.

## Role in Jardin's Security

On a world as tightly managed as Jardin, order rests on more than the regular military and the secret police. [[Social Solidarity]] watches for ideological and genetic irregularity and removes threats quietly; the [[Fort Robespierre Naval Annex]] projects military force outward. The Home Guard sits between them as the standing internal reserve — the force that turns out when unrest becomes open enough that watching is no longer enough.

Because the state expects its citizens to help defend the Perfection System, the Home Guard blurs the line between soldier and civilian. Discharged veterans keep their weapons and military IDs precisely so they can be called up during a crisis, which means that in any serious disturbance the party may face not a discrete unit but an armed, mobilized slice of the population.

## Capabilities and Limits

- Organized for rapid containment of riots, insurrection, and terrorism rather than sustained warfare.
- Equipped for street-level security — sidearms, flak vests, helmets, and networked comms — not heavy assault.
- Reinforced in an emergency by armed veterans acting under their retained military IDs.
- Operates under Social Solidarity direction at politically sensitive scenes, which can leave its rank and file caught between orders and confusion, as the Hall firefight showed.
