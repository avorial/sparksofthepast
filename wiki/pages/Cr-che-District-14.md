## Overview

Crèche District 14 is one of the largest child development complexes on Jardin.

It serves as home, school, community, and developmental center for over a million children from infancy through adolescence.

Unlike most societies, children in District 14 are not raised by biological parents.

They are raised by the State.

To most Jardinese citizens, this is entirely normal.

To foreign visitors, it is often deeply unsettling.

---

## First Impressions

Visitors expecting a grim institution are often surprised.

District 14 is beautiful.

Clean.

Quiet.

Orderly.

Safe.

Children play in parks.

Students attend classes.

Sports teams practice in open fields.

Music drifts from performance halls.

Everything appears healthy and happy.

That is what makes visitors uncomfortable.

Nothing appears wrong.

---

## Philosophy

District 14 operates under a simple belief:

> No child should be limited by the circumstances of birth.

Every child receives:

- Housing
- Nutrition
- Medical care
- Education
- Psychological support
- Recreational opportunities

The system does not view itself as replacing parents.

It views itself as replacing inequality.

---

## Structure

Children are organized into developmental cohorts called:

### Maisons

Houses.

Each Maison contains approximately:

- 120 Children
- 12 Mentors
- 8 Educational Staff
- Medical Support Personnel

Children remain with the same Maison for years.

The House becomes their family.

---

## Mentors

The closest equivalent to parents.

Mentors are highly trained professionals responsible for:

- Emotional development
- Discipline
- Guidance
- Education support

Many remain with the same cohort for decades.

Former students frequently maintain lifelong relationships with mentors.

---

## Education

District 14 contains:

- Primary Schools
- Secondary Schools
- Technical Academies
- Arts Programs
- Athletic Institutes

Educational outcomes are among the highest in the Confederation.

The district produces:

- Scientists
- Administrators
- Military Officers
- Engineers
- Artists

at remarkable rates.

---

## Daily Life

Children live structured lives.

A typical day includes:

- Education
- Physical Exercise
- Community Service
- Social Development
- Recreation

Schedules are carefully balanced.

The goal is not obedience.

The goal is optimization.

---

## The Memory Gardens

The most unusual area of District 14.

Every child possesses a personal record archive.

Messages from biological parents.

Medical records.

Academic achievements.

Personal milestones.

Upon adulthood citizens gain access to these records.

For many, this is the first time they learn much about their biological origins.

---

## Relationship with Biological Parents

Biological parents are not secret.

Nor are they central.

Most children know:

- Their genetic contributors
- Their heritage
- Their family history

But these relationships are considered secondary.

The Maison is primary.

---

## Public Opinion

Most Jardinese consider District 14 a success.

Supporters point to:

- Low crime
- Excellent education
- Social equality
- High productivity

Critics argue that something important has been lost.

Most struggle to define exactly what.

---

## Security

District 14 maintains its own branch of Social Solidarity.

Officers are rarely seen.

Problems are usually addressed long before force becomes necessary.

Visitors often describe the district as:

> "The safest place on the planet."

## What Foreigners Notice

Visitors usually report three things:

1. The children appear remarkably confident.
2. The children appear unusually independent.
3. The children rarely speak about parents.

The third observation is usually the one that stays with them.
