## Overview

The **Palais de Continuité** (Palace of Continuity) serves as both the administrative heart of Jardin and the symbolic embodiment of the world's political philosophy. Standing atop the heights overlooking central Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, the palace dominates the skyline and serves as the seat of the Supreme Presidency, the Supreme Directorate, and many of Jardin's most influential ministries.

To the citizens of Jardin, the palace represents stability, order, and the promise that each generation will improve upon the achievements of those that came before. Images of its distinctive towers appear on government broadcasts, educational materials, currency, and public monuments throughout the world.

## Session 1 Note

The transcript sometimes refers to the incident location as the Palace of Continuity, though the detailed scene takes place in the [[Hall des Futurs Choisis]] / Hall of Chosen Futures. Treat "Palace of Continuity" references in Session 1 as either broad government-complex shorthand or transcript imprecision unless later clarified.

To foreign observers, however, the palace often appears as something far more imposing: the command center of one of the most organized and closely managed societies in the Solomani Confederation.

## History

### Colonial Administration (Years 0-120)

The first government building on the site was little more than a fortified administrative center overlooking the original settlement of Sainte-Jeanne.

As the colony expanded, political authority became increasingly centralized. Successive governors added new offices, archives, and military command facilities.

### The Unification Crisis (Year 147)

Political divisions nearly fractured the young colony during the Unification Crisis. Several competing political movements sought control of the growing world.

The eventual victory of the Directorate resulted in the construction of a new governmental complex designed not merely to govern but to symbolize unity.

Construction of the original Palace of Continuity began shortly thereafter.

### The Century of Planning (Years 280-450)

As Jardin's population expanded into the billions, the palace became the center of increasingly sophisticated social planning efforts.

Entire ministries devoted to population management, education, genetics, and economic coordination established permanent headquarters within the complex.

Many of the structures visible today date from this period.

### Modern Era

Under the leadership of Supreme President Leo Duprey, extensive renovations modernized the palace's infrastructure and security systems.

Officially, these improvements were intended to improve administrative efficiency.

Unofficially, many believe the renovations included extensive underground command facilities capable of directing military and civil operations during planetary emergencies.

## Architecture

The palace complex occupies nearly six square kilometers of heavily developed government land.

The architecture reflects traditional Solomani monumental design principles:

* Massive scale
* Symmetry
* White stone facades
* Grand public spaces
* Elevated terraces
* Formal gardens
* Towering administrative spires

The most recognizable feature is the **Central Continuity Tower**, which rises nearly four hundred meters above the surrounding city.

At night, powerful illumination systems make the tower visible from nearly every district within Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne.

---

## Major Facilities

### Office of the Supreme Presidency

The official office and residence of the Supreme President.

Most executive decisions affecting Jardin ultimately pass through this office.

The Presidential Wing contains private meeting rooms, secure communications facilities, emergency command centers, and ceremonial halls.

---

### Chamber of Continuity

The most important political chamber on Jardin.

This vast circular hall hosts:

* Party Congress sessions
* National ceremonies
* State funerals
* Presidential inaugurations
* Major policy announcements

The walls contain the names of every Supreme President in the history of the world.

---

### Hall of Founders

A public museum dedicated to the early settlers of Jardin.

Schoolchildren regularly visit as part of their civic education.

The exhibits emphasize themes of sacrifice, scientific achievement, and collective responsibility.

---

### Directorate Wing

The meeting place of the Supreme Directorate.

This section contains offices for:

* National Security
* Economics
* Social Development
* Education
* Colonial Affairs
* Genetics
* Foreign Relations

Many of the most important decisions on Jardin are debated here before reaching the Presidency.

---

### Archives of Continuity

One of the largest government archives in the Confederation.

Contains:

* Historical records
* Census data
* Administrative archives
* Colonial documents
* Legislative records

Access is highly restricted.

Several sections remain permanently classified.

---

## Government Functions

The palace coordinates nearly every major function of planetary government.

These include:

### Executive Administration

Direct oversight of government agencies and ministries.

### Strategic Planning

Long-term economic, demographic, and military planning.

### Foreign Relations

Diplomatic coordination with neighboring systems and Confederation authorities.

### Emergency Management

Planetary crisis response and disaster planning.

### National Security

Coordination between military, intelligence, and internal security organizations.

## Current Leadership

### [[Leo Duprey|Supreme President Leo Duprey]]

The current ruler of Jardin.

A former Minister of Social Solidarity, Duprey rose through the Party apparatus over several decades.

Supporters credit him with:

* Economic expansion
* Political stability
* Increased military readiness
* Greater influence throughout the Pilgham region

Critics claim he has centralized too much authority within the Presidency.

Few make such criticisms publicly.

---

### [[Chief Helene Marchand|Hélène Marchand]]

**Chief of Staff**

Responsible for managing the Presidency's daily operations.

Many observers believe she controls access to power more effectively than any cabinet minister.

---

### [[Alain Mercier]]

**Minister of Genetics**

One of the most influential figures in modern Jardin politics.

His upcoming presentation at the next Party Congress has become the subject of widespread speculation throughout government and academic circles.

Many believe he is positioning himself as a future successor to Duprey.

---

## Security

Security at the Palace of Continuity is among the most extensive on the planet.

Protective measures include:

* Presidential Guard regiments
* Internal Security Directorate personnel
* Automated surveillance networks
* Biometric access controls
* Electronic counterintelligence systems
* Dedicated anti-terror units

Visitors should assume they are under continuous observation.

Most are.

---

## Daily Life

Despite its immense importance, much of the palace operates like a small city.

Each day:

* Tens of thousands of civil servants report for work.
* Diplomatic delegations arrive from across the Confederation.
* Government committees meet continuously.
* School tours move through public sections.
* Journalists attend official briefings.
* Security personnel conduct routine patrols.

The majority of the palace is devoted to administration rather than politics.

---

## Cultural Significance

Few structures carry greater symbolic importance on Jardin.

Citizens commonly view the palace as proof that civilization can be directed through careful planning and competent governance.

The palace appears regularly in:

* Educational materials
* Recruitment campaigns
* Political broadcasts
* National celebrations
* Public art

For many citizens, the palace represents the very idea of Jardin itself.

---

## Common Sayings

### "All roads lead to Continuity."

Used when discussing problems that ultimately require government involvement.

### "The Palace remembers."

A reference to the government's extensive archives and long institutional memory.

### "Ask the Tower."

A sarcastic expression used when someone expects government intervention.

---

## Rumors and Secrets

Common rumors include:

* Secret genetic archives exist beneath the palace.
* A classified command bunker extends deep into the bedrock.
* Certain citizens have personal files far larger than normal.
* Succession planning for the Presidency has already begun.
* Hidden corridors connect the palace to military facilities elsewhere in the city.

Whether any of these stories are true remains unclear.

---

## Traveller Information

Visitors are generally permitted access to public portions of the complex.

Restricted areas require:

* Official sponsorship
* Government credentials
* Security clearance

Foreign Travellers often encounter additional scrutiny.

Imperial citizens in particular may find themselves subject to extensive questioning.

Weapons are prohibited throughout most of the complex.

Violations are dealt with swiftly.

## Referee Notes

The Palace of Continuity functions best as the political center of a campaign focused on intrigue, espionage, diplomacy, and conspiracy.

Nearly every major organization on Jardin has representatives operating within the palace, making it an ideal location to introduce plots that affect the entire world.

Many campaigns may begin here but discover that the true story extends far beyond its walls.
