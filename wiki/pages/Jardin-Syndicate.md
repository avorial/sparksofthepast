# Syndicat Jardin

*Jardin Syndicate*

**Type:** Interstellar Criminal Organization

**Area of Operations:** Jardin Subsector and neighboring regions on both sides of the Solomani-Imperial border

**Primary Activities:**

* Human trafficking
* Refugee exploitation
* Identity fraud
* Smuggling
* Money laundering
* Counterfeiting
* Illegal migration
* Labor racketeering

**Threat Assessment:** High

## Session 1 Revelations

During [[Session 1 - Official Business]], [[Marcel Delphine]] described the Syndicate's genetic work as manipulation of genetic-code records to help less fortunate people acquire jobs according to merit. Emma's smuggler contact gave similar context: the Syndicate often helps people who are less genetically pure or politically disfavored by creating or altering genetic identities.

The Syndicate had feared that [[Alain Mercier]] had discovered their activity, but Marcel claimed Mercier's true finding pointed to corruption "from above." The Syndicate therefore wants the [[Mercier Frame-Up]] challenged before the official story can be used as justification for a crackdown.

## Known Assets From Session 1

- Hidden service and waste-disposal access beneath [[Arcosphere Rousseau]].
- Driverless grav vehicle extraction capability.
- [[Identity Veils]] that spoof camera recognition for approximately three cycles.
- Possible unedited footage of the assassination.
- Contacts able to recover gear while posing as cleanup or police-adjacent workers.

## Known Limits

- The Syndicate is criminal and self-interested.
- It may help the group because their survival hurts mutual enemies, not because of altruism alone.
- Marcel does not give the unedited footage freely.

## Overview

The **Jardin Syndicate** is one of the largest and most influential criminal organizations operating within the Jardin Subsector. Originally founded on Jardin itself, the Syndicate has expanded across dozens of worlds, establishing networks that span both Confederation and Imperial territory.

Unlike traditional criminal organizations, the Syndicate built its power not through narcotics, gambling, or protection rackets, but through control of migration.

The organization specializes in exploiting populations trapped between political systems.

Every year thousands of individuals seek escape from persecution, discrimination, economic hardship, or political repression. The Syndicate positions itself as a solution.

To refugees, they offer hope.

To governments, they provide plausible deniability.

To criminals, they provide opportunity.

To their victims, they are predators.

Their unofficial motto is said to be:

> "Everyone deserves a future."

The organization never specifies whose future profits from the arrangement.

---

## Origins

### The Border Opportunity

The Syndicate emerged approximately sixty years ago during a period of increasing migration between hard-line Solomani worlds and neighboring Imperial systems.

Jardin occupied a unique position.

As a prosperous world situated near the border, it served as both a destination and transit hub for displaced populations.

Certain groups found themselves particularly vulnerable:

* Non-Solomani humans
* Mixed-race populations
* Political dissidents
* Religious minorities
* Unregistered migrants
* Stateless individuals

A market emerged.

Someone was always willing to pay for transport.

Someone was always willing to sell it.

---

### The First Networks

Initially the organization consisted of independent brokers and shipping agents.

Most operated legally.

Some did not.

Over time these networks merged.

A loose confederation of smugglers, recruiters, corrupt officials, and transport operators gradually evolved into a sophisticated criminal enterprise.

The name "Jardin Syndicate" first appeared in SolSec intelligence reports nearly fifty years ago.

---

## Structure

The Syndicate operates as a decentralized network rather than a traditional hierarchy.

Cells maintain substantial autonomy.

Many members never meet senior leadership.

This structure has proven highly resistant to law-enforcement efforts.

---

### Directorate

The inner leadership circle.

Responsible for:

* Strategic planning
* Financial operations
* Political relationships
* Major smuggling routes

Few individuals know the identities of all Directorate members.

---

### Placement Agencies

The public face of the organization.

These agencies advertise:

* Employment opportunities
* Immigration assistance
* Relocation services
* Refugee support

Many are entirely legitimate.

Some are not.

Citizens rarely know the difference.

---

### Transit Networks

Responsible for moving people across interstellar borders.

Methods include:

* Commercial passenger liners
* Low berths
* Freight shipments
* False identities
* Bribed port officials

The network extends throughout the subsector.

---

### Registry Specialists

Among the Syndicate's most valuable assets.

These specialists create:

* False identities
* Forged permits
* Altered citizenship records
* Fabricated employment histories

A skilled Registry Specialist can make a person appear to have existed for decades.

---

## Business Operations

### Refugee Transport

The Syndicate's most profitable enterprise.

Clients pay for:

* Transportation
* Documentation
* Employment placement
* Housing assistance

Some arrive safely.

Others do not.

---

### Identity Laundering

The process of constructing entirely new legal identities.

Packages may include:

* Birth certificates
* Citizenship records
* Educational histories
* Employment records

Prices vary dramatically.

---

### Debt Contracts

Many migrants lack sufficient funds.

The Syndicate offers financing.

Repayment terms are often severe.

Debtors may spend years working to satisfy obligations.

---

### Labor Placement

The Syndicate maintains relationships with employers throughout the subsector.

Some placements are legitimate.

Others resemble indentured servitude.

The distinction often depends on who is asked.

---

### Counterfeiting

The organization manufactures:

* Currency
* Identification documents
* Shipping manifests
* Government certifications

High-quality counterfeit records are among its most valuable products.

---

## Political Relationships

The Syndicate survives because powerful people occasionally benefit from its activities.

---

### Corrupt Officials

Some local politicians quietly use Syndicate services.

Benefits include:

* Removal of troublesome populations
* Reduction in social tensions
* Access to unofficial labor pools

---

### Business Interests

Several corporations rely upon labor supplied through Syndicate channels.

Publicly they deny involvement.

---

### SolSec

The relationship between SolSec and the Syndicate remains complex.

Officially:

> The Syndicate is a criminal enterprise and enemy of the Confederation.

Unofficially:

Individual operations have occasionally intersected with broader political objectives.

Rumors persist that some Syndicate leaders possess information capable of embarrassing senior officials.

---

## Presence on Jardin

Jardin remains the organization's symbolic homeland.

The Syndicate's influence is strongest within:

* Rousseau Arcology
* Port Sainte-Jeanne
* Utility Canal districts
* Independent cargo facilities

Many legitimate businesses unknowingly interact with Syndicate-controlled intermediaries.

## Referee Notes

The Jardin Syndicate should never be portrayed as purely evil or purely benevolent.

They help people.

They exploit people.

They save lives.

They destroy lives.

Many citizens owe their freedom to the Syndicate.

Many others lost everything because of it.

The organization's greatest strength is that it exists in the gray space between criminality, politics, humanitarianism, and commerce.

Every faction on Jardin has a reason to hate them.

Every faction on Jardin also has a reason to quietly tolerate them.
:::
