# Les Jardins Fermés

## The Closed Gardens

**Location:** Eastern Scientific District, Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, Jardin

**Type:** Scientific Research Complex, Botanical Preserve, Agricultural Development Arcology

**Administrative Authority:** Ministry of Genetics

**Director-General:** Dr. Sophie Delacroix

**Population:** Approximately 100,000 researchers, technicians, support personnel, and students

**Public Access:** Partial

**Nickname:** *"The Gardens"*

---

# Overview

The **Jardins Fermés** (Closed Gardens) are among the greatest scientific achievements of modern Jardin and one of the most recognizable landmarks on the planet. Stretching across nearly forty square kilometers beneath a network of massive environmental domes, the Gardens serve as the primary center for biological research, agricultural innovation, ecological engineering, and environmental sciences on Jardin.

To the average citizen, the Gardens symbolize the practical benefits of scientific planning and long-term investment. Generations of breakthroughs developed within the domes have transformed Jardin into one of the most productive and self-sufficient worlds in the Solomani Confederation.

To researchers, the Gardens are a place of discovery.

To government planners, they are an investment in the future.

To foreign intelligence services, they are among the most tempting targets in the sector.

The Gardens' unofficial motto is widely known throughout Jardin:

> "The Hall Plans the Future. The Gardens Build It."

---

# History

## The Agricultural Emergency

The origins of the Gardens trace back nearly six centuries to a period known as the **Agricultural Emergency**.

Rapid industrialization and population growth placed enormous pressure on planetary food production. Although outright famine never occurred, several years of declining yields convinced government officials that dependence upon natural agricultural systems represented a long-term strategic vulnerability.

The Ministry of Development proposed a bold solution.

Rather than merely increasing farmland, Jardin would build controlled environments capable of producing food regardless of weather, disease, or environmental disruption.

The first domes were constructed shortly thereafter.

---

## The Expansion Years

Initial results exceeded expectations.

Scientists quickly developed:

* Disease-resistant grain varieties
* High-yield fruits
* Climate-adapted vegetables
* Efficient hydroponic systems
* Artificial pollination methods

Funding expanded dramatically.

Each success generated political support for additional research.

Within a century, the original agricultural station had grown into a vast scientific city.

---

## The Biological Revolution

As genetic science advanced, the Gardens became the center of biological innovation on Jardin.

Entire departments were created to study:

* Plant genetics
* Ecological engineering
* Environmental simulation
* Medical botany
* Xenobiology

Many of the technologies that define modern Jardin originated beneath the domes.

---

## The Modern Era

Today the Gardens represent one of the largest integrated biological research facilities in the Confederation.

Scientists from across Solomani space routinely seek positions within its laboratories.

Many spend entire careers inside the complex.

---

# Architecture

Unlike the monumental grandeur of the Palace of Continuity or the administrative efficiency of the Hall of Chosen Futures, the Gardens emphasize harmony between technology and nature.

The facility consists of twelve primary domes connected by enclosed transit corridors.

From orbit the complex resembles a chain of enormous crystalline bubbles stretching across the eastern edge of Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne.

Visitors entering the Gardens encounter:

* Vast indoor forests
* Artificial rivers
* Research habitats
* Botanical collections
* Climate-controlled ecosystems

Many first-time visitors compare the experience to entering another world.

---

# Major Domes

## Dome One: Conservatoire des Fondateurs

### Founders Conservatory

The primary public section of the Gardens.

Contains:

* Historical exhibits
* Public gardens
* Educational displays
* Tourist facilities

Nearly every schoolchild on Jardin visits at least once.

---

## Dome Two: Terran Heritage Preserve

### Terran Heritage Preserve

Dedicated to preserving species and ecosystems originating on Terra.

Features recreations of:

* Temperate forests
* Grasslands
* Wetlands
* Mediterranean environments

The preserve is both a research facility and a cultural monument.

---

## Dome Three: Futures Agriculture Division

### Agricultural Futures Division

The largest food-production research center on Jardin.

Researchers develop:

* New crops
* Advanced hydroponics
* Vertical farming systems
* Soil enhancement technologies

Many agricultural exports begin here.

---

## Dome Four: Atmospheric Systems Laboratory

### Atmospheric Systems Laboratory

Studies planetary climate systems.

Research includes:

* Weather control technologies
* Environmental stabilization
* Atmospheric processing
* Terraforming support methods

Several projects support Confederation colony worlds.

---

## Dome Five: Living Medicines Center

### Living Medicines Center

One of the most valuable facilities on the planet.

Specializes in:

* Pharmaceutical plants
* Medical genetics
* Bioengineered treatments
* Regenerative medicine

Numerous life-extending therapies were developed here.

---

## Dome Six: Aquatic Biosystems Facility

### Aquatic Biosystems Facility

Contains:

* Freshwater ecosystems
* Marine habitats
* Fisheries research
* Water purification studies

Several interior oceans and lakes occupy much of the dome's volume.

---

## Dome Seven: Ecological Systems Laboratory

### Ecological Systems Laboratory

Entire ecosystems are modeled within controlled environments.

Researchers study:

* Species interactions
* Population dynamics
* Environmental resilience
* Ecosystem collapse scenarios

This dome functions as a living simulation laboratory.

---

## Dome Eight: Advanced Genetic Research Division

### Advanced Genetic Research Division

One of the most prestigious scientific facilities in Jardin.

Research focuses on:

* Population genetics
* Hereditary conditions
* Long-term adaptation
* Genetic forecasting

Many projects directly support the Hall of Chosen Futures.

---

## Dome Nine: Xenobiological Annex

### Xenobiological Annex

Officially responsible for studying imported alien organisms.

Access is heavily restricted.

Most citizens know little about its operations.

Many rumors originate here.

---

## Dome Ten: Colonial Adaptation Center

### Colonial Adaptation Center

Develops biological solutions for harsh colony environments.

Projects include:

* Desert agriculture
* Arctic ecosystems
* High-gravity adaptations
* Atmospheric tolerance research

---

## Dome Eleven: Experimental Systems Division

### Experimental Systems Division

Supports unusual and high-risk research.

Most projects remain classified until completion.

---

## Dome Twelve: Restricted Research Zone

### Restricted Research Zone

Official records describe the dome only as:

> "Advanced Biological Studies."

No public tours exist.

No research summaries are released.

No scientist discusses their work there.

---

# Daily Life

The Gardens operate continuously.

Thousands of personnel travel between domes every day.

Common sights include:

* Researchers carrying specimen cases
* Students attending lectures
* Maintenance crews tending ecosystems
* Autonomous service robots
* Scientific conferences

Despite the scale of the facility, the atmosphere remains surprisingly relaxed.

Many employees describe it as a university town hidden inside a research installation.

---

# Scientific Importance

The Gardens have contributed to:

* Increased agricultural production
* Extended life expectancy
* Reduced disease rates
* Environmental restoration projects
* Colonial development programs

Many historians argue that no institution has contributed more directly to Jardin's prosperity.

---

# Public Perception

The Gardens are one of the most trusted institutions on the planet.

Citizens view them as evidence that science can improve everyday life.

Public approval remains consistently high.

A common saying states:

> "If the Gardens cannot solve it, perhaps it cannot be solved."

---

# Notable Individuals

## Dr. Sophie Delacroix

**Director-General of the Gardens**

Widely regarded as one of the leading scientific minds in the Confederation.

Frequently consulted by both Supreme President Leo Duprey and Minister Alain Mercier.

---

## Professor Lucien Morel

**Chief Ecological Systems Analyst**

Known for developing several influential ecosystem modeling theories.

Believes entire planetary biospheres can eventually be predicted mathematically.

---

## Dr. Isabelle Fournier

**Director of Medical Botany**

Responsible for multiple breakthrough therapies.

A popular public figure due to her medical achievements.

---

## Dr. Gabriel Laurent

**Director of Xenobiology**

Rarely appears in public.

His work within Dome Nine has generated considerable speculation.

---

# Security

Most of the Gardens are open and welcoming.

Some areas are not.

Security is provided by:

* Garden Security Directorate
* Ministry of Genetics Security Branch
* Internal Security Bureau

Visitors quickly notice certain corridors and transit lines are inaccessible.

Questions about these restrictions are politely ignored.

---

# Rumors

## The Silent Forest

An ecosystem hidden within a restricted section of Dome Twelve.

Researchers allegedly enter but rarely discuss what they observed.

---

## Mercier's Laboratory

Many believe Alain Mercier maintains a private research facility somewhere beneath the Gardens.

No evidence has ever surfaced.

---

## The Perfect Crop

A genetically engineered plant capable of eliminating food shortages across hundreds of worlds.

The government supposedly refuses to release it.

---

## Living Archive

Some claim the Gardens preserve biological samples from every major family line on Jardin.

Officials deny this.

---

## The Lost Species

Several supposedly extinct Terran species are rumored to survive within hidden sections of the complex.

---

# Classified Information

## Project Verdant Horizon

A highly classified initiative combining ecological forecasting with demographic modeling.

Researchers attempt to predict how biological systems and human populations will evolve over centuries.

Only a handful of senior officials possess access.

---

## Dome Twelve

Contrary to public records, Dome Twelve is not a single facility.

It contains an entire network of subterranean laboratories extending beneath the Gardens.

Even many senior researchers are unaware of the complex's full extent.

---

## The Mercier Findings

Several portions of Minister Alain Mercier's controversial upcoming Party Congress presentation originated from research conducted within the Gardens.

What those findings reveal remains unknown.

---

# Referee Notes

The Gardens represent the optimistic face of Jardin.

Unlike the Palace, which represents authority, or the Hall, which represents administration, the Gardens demonstrate tangible achievements.

Visitors should leave impressed.

The facility should feel inspiring rather than sinister.

Only after spending time there should players begin to notice the locked doors, missing reports, classified projects, and unanswered questions hidden beneath the beauty of the domes.

The greatest secrets on Jardin are not hidden in the Palace.

They grow beneath glass.
