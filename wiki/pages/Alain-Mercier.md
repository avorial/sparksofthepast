# Alain Mercier

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Alain Étienne Mercier
**Species:** Human (Solomani)
**Citizenship:** Solomani Confederation (Jardin)
**Birth Year:** 1061
**Birthplace:** Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, Jardin
**Occupation:** Minister of Genetics, Government of Jardin
**Political Affiliation:** Solomani Party (Technocratic Reform Bloc)
**Status:** Deceased (1105)

---

## Overview

Alain Mercier was a Jardinese geneticist, civil servant, and politician who served as Minister of Genetics during the administration of Supreme President Leo Duprey. Widely regarded as one of the foremost experts on population genetics and demographic analysis within the Solomani Confederation, Mercier oversaw the Ministry of Genetics during a period of unprecedented expansion in genetic research and data collection.

Although generally viewed as a loyal supporter of the Solomani Cause, Mercier developed a reputation for placing scientific findings above political convenience. His insistence on evidence-based policy earned him respect among researchers and academics but often placed him at odds with powerful political interests.

Mercier became a figure of subsector-wide interest shortly before his death when he announced plans to release the results of a major long-term demographic study at the upcoming Solomani Party Congress.

---

## Early Life

Born in the capital city of Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, Mercier was the son of two senior administrators employed by Jardin's Eugenics Bureau. Identified at an early age as possessing exceptional intellectual aptitude, he was selected for advanced educational programs and graduated with distinction from the Jardin Institute of Biological Sciences.

His academic work focused on long-term population stability, hereditary trait distribution, and the interaction between social planning and genetic outcomes. Several of his early papers became required reading within Confederation genetics programs.

---

## Academic Career

Before entering government service, Mercier spent nearly two decades as a researcher and lecturer. He gained prominence through his work analyzing the effects of large-scale demographic interventions across multiple generations.

His findings challenged several accepted assumptions regarding population management and brought him to the attention of senior Party officials. Unlike many researchers who remained within academia, Mercier demonstrated an unusual ability to communicate complex scientific findings to political leaders.

By his mid-forties he had become one of the Confederation's most influential voices on genetics policy.

---

## Minister of Genetics

Mercier was appointed Minister of Genetics in 1097 following the retirement of his predecessor.

During his tenure the Ministry expanded several major initiatives, including:

* Confederation-wide genetic archive modernization.
* Longitudinal population studies.
* Enhanced hereditary disease screening.
* Interplanetary demographic monitoring.
* Research into population resilience and long-term genetic stability.

Although publicly supportive of existing policy, Mercier repeatedly argued that no social system could remain effective unless it was subjected to continual scientific review.

His ministry became known for its unusually rigorous standards and independence from factional politics.

---

## Political Views

Mercier was often described as a technocrat rather than an ideologue.

While loyal to Jardin and the Solomani Cause, he frequently emphasized that scientific institutions should not be influenced by political considerations.

His most famous public statement was delivered shortly before his death:

> "We owe it to the citizens of Jardin to follow the data wherever it leads. The strength of our society has always been built upon scientific rigor, honest evaluation, and the courage to improve."

Supporters viewed this philosophy as evidence of intellectual integrity.

Critics argued that it demonstrated political naivety.

---

## Final Months

In the final year of his life, Mercier directed a comprehensive review of demographic and genetic data collected over multiple generations.

At a press conference held at the Hall of Genetics, he announced that his ministry would reveal significant findings at the upcoming Solomani Party Congress.

The announcement generated intense speculation throughout political, military, and academic circles.

Observers noted that Mercier's remarks were unusually direct and appeared to suggest conclusions with potentially far-reaching implications.

## Session 1 - Assassination

During [[Session 1 - Official Business]], the characters saw a news broadcast reporting that Mercier would reveal corruption in the genetic-affairs files at the coming Party Congress. The broadcast framed the announcement as potentially revolutionary for the government.

Shortly afterward, Mercier rushed through the office area at the [[Hall des Futurs Choisis]] and was killed by multiple shots. The scene appeared designed to make the player characters look responsible: several characters detected signs that their own weapons had moved, fired, or been made to appear as if they fired.

Items recovered from Mercier:

- [[Mercier's Locked Pad]]
- His phone.
- Concealed armor, which failed to stop the barrage.

[[Marcel Delphine]] later claimed Mercier's real discovery pointed to corruption "from above," not to the [[Jardin Syndicate]]'s genetic identity manipulation.

## Death

On 143-1105, while attending official business at the Hall of Genetics in Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, Mercier was assassinated.

The murder shocked Jardin and rapidly became one of the most significant criminal and political investigations in recent Confederation history.

His death occurred only weeks before the scheduled release of his ministry's report.

The contents of the report have never been publicly disclosed.

---

## Legacy

Alain Mercier remains a controversial figure in Jardinese history.

To supporters, he embodied the principle that scientific truth should guide public policy regardless of political consequences.

To critics, he represented a dangerous willingness to question institutions that had maintained stability for centuries.

His unfinished work continues to be the subject of speculation among historians, political analysts, and genetic researchers throughout the Solomani Confederation.
