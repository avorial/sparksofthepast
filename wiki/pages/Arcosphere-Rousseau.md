# Arcosphère Rousseau

## Rousseau Arcology

**Location:** Central Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, Jardin

**Type:** Urban Arcology, Residential Megastructure, Commercial Hub

**Administrative Authority:** Arcology Administration Directorate

**Governor:** Adrien Pelletier

**Population:** 1.8 Billion Permanent Residents

**Daily Population:** Approximately 2.3 Billion

**Public Access:** Open

**Nickname:** *"Rousseau"*

---

# Overview

The **Arcosphère Rousseau** (Rousseau Arcology) is the largest structure on Jardin and one of the most densely populated urban environments in the Solomani Confederation.

Dominating the center of Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne, the arcology forms a massive crescent surrounding the Grand Axis and serves as the residential, commercial, industrial, and cultural heart of the planetary capital.

To outsiders, Rousseau appears impossible.

To residents, it is simply home.

Nearly one in seven citizens of Jardin lives within its walls.

Entire generations are born, educated, employed, married, and buried without ever leaving the arcology.

Government planners proudly describe Rousseau as:

> "Human civilization perfected."

Residents typically describe it as:

> "Crowded."

---

# History

## The Arcology Initiative

Construction began nearly two centuries ago during the Great Expansion.

Rapid population growth threatened to consume valuable agricultural land surrounding Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne.

Rather than expand outward indefinitely, planners chose a different solution.

The city would expand upward.

The Arcology Initiative combined:

* Housing
* Industry
* Transportation
* Commerce
* Education
* Recreation

into a single integrated structure.

The project became one of the largest construction efforts in Confederation history.

---

## The Rousseau Doctrine

Named after the ancient Terran philosopher Jean-Jacques Rousseau, the arcology was intended to demonstrate that careful planning could create a superior urban environment.

Government publications described it as:

> "A city designed for citizens rather than automobiles."

The original plans envisioned housing approximately 800 million residents.

Reality proved more complicated.

---

## The Quiet Expansion

Over successive generations, unused maintenance spaces, storage sections, and service corridors were gradually converted into residential and commercial districts.

Some expansions were legal.

Others were not.

The result is a structure far larger than official plans ever intended.

No one knows its true size.

---

# Architecture

From above, Rousseau resembles an immense crescent embracing the Grand Axis of Nouvelle Sainte-Jeanne.

The structure rises hundreds of meters above the city and extends deep beneath ground level.

Major features include:

* Residential towers
* Commercial districts
* Internal parks
* Transit networks
* Schools
* Hospitals
* Manufacturing centers

The arcology contains enough internal infrastructure to function independently for months if isolated.

---

# Major Districts

## Les Sommets

### The Summit Levels

The highest and most prestigious districts.

Home to:

* Senior Party officials
* Corporate executives
* Leading scientists
* Wealthy professionals

Features include:

* Private sky gardens
* Luxury apartments
* Exclusive clubs
* Fine dining establishments

Many apartments possess direct views of the Palace of Continuity.

Residents insist this is merely coincidence.

---

## Les Niveaux Civiques

### Civic Levels

The professional middle class.

Home to:

* Teachers
* Engineers
* Doctors
* Administrators
* Researchers

These districts represent the idealized vision of Jardin society.

Clean.

Efficient.

Safe.

Almost every government promotional video is filmed here.

---

## Les Communes

### Commons Levels

The largest section of Rousseau.

Home to more than a billion residents.

Contains:

* Housing blocks
* Schools
* Markets
* Restaurants
* Recreation facilities

This is where most ordinary citizens live.

Visitors often compare it to a city existing entirely indoors.

---

## Les Ateliers

### Industrial Levels

The manufacturing heart of the arcology.

Contains:

* Factories
* Warehouses
* Robot maintenance facilities
* Recycling systems
* Cargo handling centers

Thousands of freight vehicles move through the district every hour.

Most visitors never see it.

Most residents depend upon it.

---

## Les Profondeurs

### The Depths

The lowest officially recognized districts.

Contains:

* Utility systems
* Transit infrastructure
* Water processing facilities
* Power distribution networks

Officially sparsely populated.

Unofficially not.

---

# The Unofficial City

## Les Ombres

### The Shadows

The name given to unregistered districts hidden within the arcology.

These areas do not officially exist.

Generations of unauthorized construction have created:

* Informal neighborhoods
* Hidden marketplaces
* Illegal workshops
* Refugee communities
* Smuggling routes

Authorities periodically deny their existence.

Everyone else knows better.

---

# Daily Life

Life inside Rousseau rarely requires leaving the structure.

Residents have access to:

* Employment
* Education
* Entertainment
* Healthcare
* Transportation

within walking distance.

Many citizens spend months without seeing open sky.

Artificial lighting systems simulate natural day-night cycles.

Most residents stop noticing.

---

# Transportation

Rousseau possesses one of the most advanced transit systems in the Confederation.

Transportation methods include:

* Maglev trains
* Moving pedestrian corridors
* Autonomous shuttles
* Freight elevators
* Grav lifts

The average resident can reach any major district within thirty minutes.

---

# Commerce

The arcology contains millions of businesses.

Everything from:

* Street vendors
* Family restaurants
* Boutique manufacturers
* Corporate headquarters

can be found within Rousseau.

Many of the Confederation's largest corporations maintain offices here.

---

# Culture

Rousseau possesses its own distinct identity.

Residents often describe themselves as:

> "Rousseans first, Jardinais second."

Whether this is intended as a joke remains unclear.

The arcology has developed:

* Unique slang
* Local traditions
* Distinct cuisine
* Neighborhood rivalries

Many citizens never fully identify with the broader city.

---

# Major Landmarks

## Forum Rousseau

The central public square.

Location of:

* Public speeches
* Demonstrations
* Cultural festivals

The largest indoor gathering space on Jardin.

---

## Market of a Thousand Worlds

A sprawling commercial district.

Contains goods from across the Confederation and beyond.

One of the best places on Jardin to find something unusual.

---

## The People's Conservatory

A vast indoor park.

Contains:

* Lakes
* Forests
* Walking paths

Many residents visit simply to experience something resembling nature.

---

## The Arcology Museum

Documents the history of Rousseau and urban development throughout the Confederation.

Popular with school groups.

Not popular with schoolchildren.

---

# Security

Public security is maintained by:

* Arcology Security Directorate
* Municipal Police
* SolSec liaison officers

Security levels vary considerably between districts.

The Summit Levels are among the safest places on Jardin.

The Shadows are not.

---

# Criminal Activity

Despite government claims, Rousseau possesses a substantial underworld.

Activities include:

* Smuggling
* Identity fraud
* Unregistered robotics
* Counterfeiting
* Information theft

Most criminal activity occurs within The Shadows.

---

# The Jardin Syndicate

The arcology serves as the primary operational center of the Jardin Syndicate.

Officially this is false.

Everyone knows it is true.

Numerous front organizations operate within the Commons and Industrial districts.

Their influence is particularly strong among:

* Migrant populations
* Refugee communities
* Unregistered residents

The Syndicate's unofficial slogan is frequently heard in the Shadows:

> "Everything is possible. It simply isn't approved."

---

# Referee Notes

Rousseau should feel alive.

Unlike the Palace of Continuity, which represents authority, or the Hall of Chosen Futures, which represents administration, Rousseau represents people.

It contains everything.

Success and failure.

Wealth and poverty.

Order and chaos.

Idealism and corruption.

If the Palace is the mind of Jardin and the Hall is its nervous system, then Rousseau is its beating heart.

Most campaigns set on Jardin will eventually spend more time here than anywhere else.

The arcology is large enough to contain an entire campaign by itself.
