## Overview

The **Hall des Futurs Choisis** (Hall of Chosen Futures) is among the most influential institutions on Jardin and arguably the most important government building outside the Palace of Continuity itself.

Officially, the Hall exists to coordinate the scientific, educational, demographic, and civic planning efforts that have guided Jardin's development for centuries.

In practice, the Hall serves as the point where government policy intersects with everyday life.

Every citizen on Jardin will interact with the Hall repeatedly throughout their lifetime.

Birth registrations, educational assessments, citizenship certifications, genetic consultations, robot registrations, family planning permits, immigration reviews, military aptitude evaluations, and countless other services are administered through its vast bureaucracy.

For many citizens, the Hall represents the triumph of science and reason over chaos.

For many foreigners, it represents something far more unsettling.

---

## Public Purpose

The Hall's official mission is inscribed above the main entrance:

> "The Future Is Not Found. It Is Built."

The institution exists to help citizens make informed decisions that contribute to both personal success and societal prosperity.

Government publications describe the Hall as:

* A center of scientific guidance
* A guardian of public welfare
* A steward of future generations
* A repository of civic records
* A facilitator of social opportunity

Critics throughout the Confederation often describe it as:

* A surveillance apparatus
* A demographic control center
* A social engineering institution

Most citizens reject such characterizations.

---

## History

### Foundation Era

The original Hall began as a simple census and population records office during Jardin's first century.

As population expanded, government planners discovered that accurate demographic forecasting provided significant economic advantages.

Successive administrations gradually expanded the office's authority.

What began as a registry evolved into a planning bureau.

The planning bureau evolved into a ministry.

The ministry eventually became the Hall.

---

### The Genetic Reforms

The modern Hall emerged during the Genetic Reforms of the sixth century.

Advances in biological sciences allowed officials to:

* Predict hereditary disorders
* Track population trends
* Improve public health outcomes
* Guide educational development

These programs proved remarkably successful.

Life expectancy increased.

Crime declined.

Educational outcomes improved.

The public generally supported further expansion.

---

### The Integration Period

During the following centuries dozens of independent agencies were merged into the Hall.

Functions absorbed included:

* Birth registration
* Citizenship management
* Immigration processing
* Marriage licensing
* Educational placement
* Robot registration
* Population statistics
* Genetic counseling

By the modern era, nearly every major life event passed through the Hall.

---

## Architecture

The Hall covers nearly four square kilometers and consists of seven interconnected administrative towers surrounding a central civic plaza.

Unlike the grandeur of the Palace of Continuity, the Hall was designed to appear approachable.

Its architecture emphasizes:

* Transparency
* Efficiency
* Accessibility
* Scientific progress

Visitors enter through enormous public atriums filled with:

* Trees
* Gardens
* Public art
* Information kiosks
* Educational exhibits

The atmosphere resembles a university campus more than a government ministry.

Only after proceeding deeper into the complex does its true scale become apparent.

## Major Divisions

### Citizen Registry Directorate

The oldest department within the Hall.

Responsible for maintaining records on every legal resident of Jardin.

Files contain:

* Birth records
* Family history
* Educational records
* Employment history
* Military service
* Citizenship classification

Many records extend back generations.

Officials proudly describe the Registry as the most complete demographic database in the Confederation.

---

### Bureau of Genetic Assessment

Perhaps the Hall's most famous department.

Provides:

* Genetic screening
* Health forecasting
* Hereditary risk analysis
* Family planning consultation

Participation is technically voluntary.

Nearly everyone participates.

Citizens often view refusing assessment as irresponsible.

---

### Directorate of Educational Futures

Tracks educational development from childhood through adulthood.

Responsibilities include:

* Aptitude testing
* School placement
* Scholarship recommendations
* Professional forecasting

Students are not assigned careers.

However, guidance recommendations are considered highly reliable.

Many citizens follow them.

---

### Bureau of Family Development

Handles:

* Marriage registrations
* Family planning consultations
* Adoption reviews
* Child development programs

This bureau generates much of the Hall's reputation outside Jardin.

Imperial media frequently exaggerates its role.

In reality, most services remain advisory rather than mandatory.

---

### Immigration and Citizenship Authority

Responsible for:

* Immigration processing
* Naturalization
* Residency permits
* Foreign worker authorization

Every long-term visitor eventually encounters this department.

---

### Robot Registration Bureau

One of the Hall's newer divisions.

Every autonomous robot on Jardin must be registered.

Records include:

* Ownership
* Operational classification
* Maintenance history
* Intelligence certification
* Security reviews

Visitors arriving with robots are required to register them within seven days.

Military-grade machines receive additional scrutiny.

Unregistered robots can be confiscated.

### Birth

---

---

### Retirement

Medical support planning.

Historical record preservation.

Legacy documentation.

## Daily Operations

Nearly 500,000 people pass through the Hall every week.

Despite this volume, waiting times remain remarkably short.

Visitors frequently report:

* Professional staff
* Efficient processing
* Friendly service
* Exceptional organization

Foreigners often find the experience unnerving.

Questions seem prepared before they are asked.

Records appear instantly.

Appointments begin precisely on schedule.

Officials already know what forms are required.

---

## Minister Alain Mercier

Current Minister of Genetics and director of the Hall.

Mercier is widely considered one of the most influential individuals on Jardin.

Educated at the National Academy of Biological Sciences, he spent decades overseeing demographic planning initiatives before ascending to ministerial leadership.

Supporters view him as:

* A visionary scientist
* A reformer
* A defender of evidence-based policy

Critics accuse him of:

* Excessive technocratic thinking
* Overreliance on predictive systems
* Expanding state influence into private life

His upcoming presentation at the next Party Congress has generated enormous public interest.

---

## Public Opinion

Surveys consistently show strong public approval.

Citizens commonly cite:

* Efficient services
* Improved healthcare
* Better educational outcomes
* Reduced bureaucracy

A common Jardin saying states:

> "The Hall knows where the forms are."

Meaning that even complicated problems usually have a solution.

## Security

Security is extensive but intentionally discreet.

Visible measures include:

* Civil security officers
* Automated scanners
* Visitor registration systems

Less visible measures include:

* Internal intelligence personnel
* Data monitoring teams
* Behavioral analysis systems
* Counterespionage units

The Hall maintains one of the most valuable information repositories in the Confederation.

---

## Foreign Visitors

Visitors remaining longer than seven days must register.

The process is usually straightforward.

Typical requirements include:

* Identity verification
* Ship registration
* Residence declaration
* Robot registration
* Temporary citizen file creation

Most visitors complete the process within an hour.

Many leave feeling uncomfortable without being entirely certain why.

## Rumors

Numerous rumors surround the Hall.

Common stories include:

* The government maintains predictive profiles on every citizen.
* Secret archives contain centuries of genetic data.
* Certain future leaders are identified during childhood.
* The Hall can predict career success with startling accuracy.
* Hidden departments monitor social stability across the planet.

Officials dismiss such stories as exaggerations.

## Classified Information

The following information is not publicly known.

### Project Horizon

A classified demographic forecasting initiative operating beneath the Hall.

Project Horizon attempts to model Jardin's future decades in advance.

Economic, educational, political, and biological data are continuously analyzed.

Officially, the project does not exist.

---

### Restricted Archives

Deep beneath the Hall lies a secure repository containing genetic records dating back centuries.

Access is limited to a handful of senior officials.

Even many Hall administrators are unaware of its existence.

---

### The Mercier Findings

Recent research conducted under Minister Alain Mercier has produced highly controversial results.

The exact nature of these findings remains unknown.

However, rumors have spread throughout government circles that the data may challenge long-standing assumptions underlying Jardin's social planning philosophy.

Several senior officials reportedly view the upcoming Party Congress with considerable anxiety.

:::

## Adventure Hooks

### Missing File

A citizen's entire history vanishes from the Registry.

Officially this should be impossible.

### Ghost Citizen

Records indicate a person who has never existed.

Yet dozens of government databases recognize them.

### Stolen Genome

A classified genetic profile is stolen from the Restricted Archives.

Several intelligence agencies become interested.

### Foreign Defector

A Hall researcher seeks passage off-world.

They claim to possess evidence capable of destabilizing the government.

### Robot Uprising

Thousands of robot registrations suddenly contain identical authorization codes.

No one can explain why.

### The Wrong Future

A prominent citizen receives a predictive assessment describing events that have not yet happened.

Months later, those events begin occurring.

### Party Congress

The Travellers become entangled in the political fallout surrounding Alain Mercier's long-awaited presentation.

### Session 1 - Official Business

The player characters were summoned here for administrative reasons tied to genetic status, military records, finances, or autonomous AI review. The process resembled a large bureaucratic office: numbers were assigned, people were called in strange orders, and military IDs were checked because many arrived armed.

The grouping was suspicious. Too many armed veterans and connected figures were brought together in one area, and several already knew each other from prior service.

Shortly after a news broadcast about [[Alain Mercier]] and genetic-file corruption, Mercier crossed the office area and was killed in the [[Mercier Assassination]].

### Session 1 Security Notes

- The Hall had cameras throughout the office area.
- Government workers used bioscans to open secure side doors.
- [[Home Guard]] and [[Social Solidarity]] personnel responded quickly.
- A walker/mech later breached the window during the group's escape.

### Session 2 - Aftermath Recon

The next day, the party returned under [[Identity Veils]] and found the exterior repairs already underway. Scaffolding covered the wall damaged during the walker escape, and security preparation was visible around the nearby Palace of Continuity in anticipation of protests or unrest.

[[Social Solidarity]] personnel were blocking [[Inspector Marc Valois]] and [[SolSec]] from accessing the assassination scene. Inside the Hall, [[Dr. Luc Moreau]] tried to send data out through the plumbing in a [[Mobile Data Tube]]. The party intercepted it and later recovered part of the [[Mercier Genetic Findings]].

## Referee Notes

The Hall des Futurs Choisis should not be portrayed as overtly sinister.

The institution is most effective when it appears competent, rational, and largely beneficial.

The discomfort comes not from oppression but from capability.

Everything works.

The records are accurate.

The recommendations are useful.

The staff are courteous.

The government genuinely improves many lives.

The question facing visitors is whether any institution should possess this much information, this much influence, and this much confidence in its ability to shape the future.

That question lies at the heart of modern Jardin.

:::
