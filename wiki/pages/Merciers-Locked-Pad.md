# Mercier's Locked Pad

*The minister's last secret, one bad guess from ash*

## Overview

Mercier's locked pad was recovered from [[Alain Mercier]] after his assassination. It is roughly Kindle-sized and password protected, and it is almost certainly the reason he was killed with it still on his body — the killers wanted him silenced, not searched.

## The Lock

The pad is protected by a wipe-on-failure lock: **five wrong password attempts destroy the data permanently.** One wrong attempt has already been spent, which leaves **four tries** before the contents are gone for good. There is no way to tell from the outside how close a guess is, and a failed brute force earlier confirmed the counter is real rather than a bluff.

That single burned attempt makes the pad as dangerous as it is valuable. Whoever opens it has to be right, and being wrong is not a delay — it is the loss of the only physical evidence Mercier left behind.

## Known Facts

- The pad was on Mercier's body when he was killed.
- An early hack attempt failed and burned one of the five tries.
- Four attempts remain before the pad wipes itself.
- Its contents are unknown to the party.
- At the end of [[Session 2 - Masks and Markers]], the group still intends to revisit the pad, but has not opened it.

## Importance

The pad is widely assumed to hold the evidence Mercier meant to present at the [[Friday Party Congress Hearing]] — the genetic findings the official record has since worked so hard to corrupt and bury. What is actually on it goes further than anyone at the table yet knows.

## GM Truth — What the Pad Contains

The pad is Mercier's private working file, kept off every official system precisely because of what it proves. Cracking it reveals, in roughly this order:

- **There is a Psi group inside [[Social Solidarity]].** A hidden cadre operating behind the ministry's Office of Exceptional Citizens and Office of Social Forecasting — the same people who are supposed to *manage* extraordinary citizens are quietly composed of them. Mercier's notes state plainly that they are **"not fully human"**: living carriers of the very markers (HX-14, SC-9, MY22, PX-4) his research tied to psionic-like ability in the [[Mercier Genetic Findings]].

- **They took power by inverting the Perfection System.** The Psi group has climbed by **lying that high-ranking officials carry bad genetics** — fabricating "defect" records for rivals and then presenting themselves as the discreet fixers who will keep the shameful results quiet. The threat of a manufactured genetic scandal, and the "favor" of covering it up, is the leverage that has put their people into position across the government.

- **They have been hoarding the markers they call defects.** The same HX-/SC-/MY-/PX- markers the group brands as flaws in their enemies, they have been quietly **concentrating in their own bloodlines** for two generations. The Perfection System's stated goal and the Psi group's actual breeding program are opposites wearing the same uniform.

- **The assassination was a demonstration, not just a silencing.** The "impossible" behavior of the weapons at the [[Mercier Assassination]] — rounds that curved, guns that spoofed — was the Psi group showing what it can do, then pinning it on the party through the [[Mercier Frame-Up]]. Mercier suspected this before he died and says so.

- **A short list of the ruined.** The pad names several officials the group has already broken or is actively blackmailing with false genetic records, including at least one figure close to the Supreme Presidency. It does **not** confirm whether [[Leo Duprey]] is a protector of the group, a target of it, or unaware — Mercier ran out of time.

- **It is partly a message.** The final entries are addressed to whoever opens the pad. Mercier expected to be killed and left instructions: trust the markers, not the ministry; and assume that the act of cracking the pad may itself trip a flag the Psi group is watching for.

**Running it:** the five-strike wipe is the whole tension — the players will want to bring the pad to someone who can crack it ([[Lucin]], a Syndicate specialist, [[Inspector Marc Valois]]'s people) and every such contact is a chance for the Psi group to hear about it. A wrong guess doesn't just fail; it destroys the campaign's cleanest proof and hands the group's future entirely to unreliable testimony.
